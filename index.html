Moomin World

Moomin World

Jordyn Czyzewski

This style, called Moomin World, turns comic illustrations of Moomin and friends into a multiscale basemap.

The Moomin comics were created in the mid-twentieth century by Finnish artist and writer Tove Jansson.

The stories follow a gentle troll family and their friends as they navigate outdoor adventures in the changing Nordic landscape.

Rooted deeply in Finnish culture, the comics draw on the region’s forests, coastlines, and folklore, which gives the Moomin world its unmistakable sense of place.

The comics are a distinct part of the larger Moomin franchise, which also includes novels and animated series, which have helped make the Moomins one of Finland’s most recognizable cultural icons.

My map aligns thematically with the adventures of Moomin world, focusing on natural outdoor features and paths opposed to building structures and city forms, to align with the story.

Inspiration to create elements within this Moomin-styled map came from four pillars of the style: Form, Color, Type, and Texture, all of which helped translate the charm of Tove Janssons' illustrations into a functional and playful cartographic design.

Form: I wanted to focus on rounded shapes and feelings of mixed hand drawn textures, integrating a soft feel and playful linework.

Color: Colors of this fantasy world include dark green vegetation and golden yellow roads, contrasting hues blend together for a whimsical feel. Dotted moss green land cover aim to feel like a page pulled from a Moomin comic book immersing the viewer into the story.

Type: I took inspiration from the blocky, uppercase letters of the uniquely designed Moomin font along with the eloquent handwritten script by Jansson to integrate into labels.

Texture: Dense linework for teal and blue oceans adding a sketch-like feel and bold contrasting hues were used for bodies of water, inspired by this Moomin comic image.

Moomin faces mark populated areas. Larger-sized Moomins represent bigger cities, while smaller ones note less dense settlements. White circles around the Moomin echo comic speech bubbles and keep the icons visible above dense forests and textured water.

The map changes and develops more detail as you zoom in.

At the broadest global scale, teal seas and dark continents carry almost all of the visual weight.

Moving in to the overview of Northern Europe, coastlines and fjords start to show the sketchy ocean texture.

The Helsinki region reveals stippled forests, rounded land use patches, and warm yellow main roads.

Regional views like Wisconsin balance dense forests, lakes, and Moomin city markers.

Zooming all the way in, individual streets, parks, and shorelines break into detailed linework.

We start at the scale of the whole world. Oceans dominate the frame, filled with light aqua strokes that mimic ink hatching in the original Moomin illustrations.

Moving in to Northern Europe, the palette focuses on teal seas and deep forests, situating the Moomin world around the Nordic coastline.

Looking closer into Southern Finland and Northern Estonia, country and water labeling along with city icon size varies at a closer scale.

At the Helsinki metro scale, islands, peninsulas, and forests share space with a golden road network. The warm roads echo printed ink lines on top of flat color plates.

Inside central neighborhoods, stippled land cover, yellow roads, and location names carry most of the detail.

Now that we have looked at the changes across scales, let's view how varied geographies impact the map display.

The glaciated region of northern Iceland shows how the terrain style works. Sketchy hillshade strokes follow valleys and ridges, giving the mountains a carved, storybook feel.

Across the United States, teal lakes, dark forests, and pale stippling hold the style together at continental scale while Moomin icons pick out key cities.

Keystone, Colorado reveals the varied terrain hues on alpine slopes. Forest dots and hillshade lines wrap around the ski runs and village without losing legibility.

A view of Wisconsin demonstrates state-level features, with county and state boundaries defined along with prominent cities.

Finally, the tour ends in central Madison, demonstrating what the campus and downtown area might look like in Moomin style, with plenty of land cover for the characters to run around and have fun outdoor adventures by the lakes.
